Rhubarb and Coconut Crumble

Comforting Crumble

A cosy treat with a subtle coconutty surprise. Not too heavy, this crumble is true comfort food and will challenge you not to go back for more. Especially delicous when your rhubarb is at its most tart to highlight the contrast with the coconut. When I first came across this Swedish recipe from Receptfavoriter I was hooked. You can also make it with potato flour.

Ingredients

  • Rhubarb - 700g
  • Granulated sugar - 1/2 dl
  • Butter - 125g
  • Wheat flour - 1.5 dl
  • Sugar - 1.5 dl
  • Coconut flakes - 1 dl
  • Oats - 1 dl

Steps

  1. Warm the oven to 200°C.
  2. Peel and roughly shred the rhubarb.
  3. Grease a pie case of about a 24 cm diameter with a little butter.
  4. Spread the rhubard out on the bottom of the pie case.
  5. Sprinkle 0.5 dl of the sugar across this.
  6. Cut the butter into small pieces and put it in a bowl. Knead it quickly together with the flour, remaining sugar, coconut and oats to form a crumbly texture.
  7. Spread the crumble out evenly over the rhubarb.
  8. Bake the rhubarb pie for 35 minutes in the middle of the oven or until it has a nice colour.
  9. Take it out and let it cool a little before serving with vanilla custard or icecream. Alternatively enjoy cold.
